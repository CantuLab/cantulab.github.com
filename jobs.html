Postdoctoral Position in Comparative Genomics of Wild Grapes (posted on June 22 2018)

The Cantu lab in the Department of Viticulture & Enology at UC Davis (http://cantulab.github.io) is seeking a Postdoctoral Research fellow to work on whole genome analysis and comparative genomics of wild grape species. The Cantu lab has initiated the sequencing of selected accessions of wild grape species that tolerate biotic and abiotic stresses using a combination of single molecule real time sequencing and long-range scaffolding methods. Diploid whole genome information is being complemented with resequencing data to explore genetic diversity among accessions and transcriptomic data to profile gene expression during development as well as facilitate gene predictions. The selected candidate will be responsible of whole-genome and transcriptome assemblies, sequence and structural variation analyses, and genome-wide association studies. The postdoc will be expected to draft reports and papers for publication in peer-reviewed journals. The postdoc will work with wet lab personnel as well as bioinformaticians in the lab and in close collaboration with the laboratory of Brandon Gaut at UC Irvine.

Applicants should have demonstrated expertise in bioinformatics, preferably genomic analysis, as evidenced by peer-reviewed publications as well as a strong computational background and effective oral and written communication skills in English.

Applicants should send a cover letter, resume, and contact information for three professional references as a single pdf to: dacantu@ucdavis.edu. The position is available immediately and is open until filled.
